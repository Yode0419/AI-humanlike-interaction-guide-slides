# 考量人類化互動的期望

## Understanding Anthropomorphism & Human-likeness in AI
## 理解 AI 中的擬人化與人類化

---

### Anthropomorphization | Humanization | Embodied AI
### 擬人化 | 人類化 | 具身 AI

**Anthropomorphization（擬人化）**
Ascribing attributes traditionally understood as human to non-human objects, concepts, machines, experiences, and animals. E.g. Agency, consciousness, intention, personality, awareness, or sociality. This is a default human behavior that isn't predicated on the presence of anthropomorphic cues.

將傳統上被視為人類專屬的特質，賦予非人類的物體、概念、機器、體驗與動物。例如：能動性、意識、意圖、個性、感知能力或社交性。這是人類的預設行為，不需要擬人化線索的存在才會發生。

---

**Humanization（人類化）**
Intentionally adding human-like characteristics and features into the design and building of non-human objects, concepts, machines, and experiences. E.g. Facial features, human identity, phatic responses, filler pauses, or language generation. This can support relatability and improve usability to some extent.

刻意將人類特徵與功能，加入非人類物體、概念、機器與體驗的設計與建構中。例如：臉部特徵、人類身份識別、寒暄應答、停頓語氣詞或語言生成能力。這在一定程度上可以提升親切感並改善可用性。

---

**Embodied AI（具身 AI）**
AI systems that are designed to interact physically in virtual (e.g. game-based scenes) and real environments (e.g. robotics) and learn from these interactions with the environment around them, similar to how humans learn.

設計用來在虛擬（如遊戲場景）與現實環境（如機器人）中進行實體互動，並從與周遭環境的互動中學習的 AI 系統，類似於人類的學習方式。

---

### Anthropomorphization is our tendency to ascribe human attributes to non-human entities
### 擬人化是我們將人類特質投射到非人類實體的傾向

People, by default, tend to ascribe human characteristics and behaviors to non-human entities like animals, objects, and even technologies. This tendency is known as anthropomorphism. We don't anthropomorphize a system, object, or animal because we truly believe it to have human characteristics. Rather, this is an automatic cognitive response that allows us to predict its behavior and guide our interactions with it.

人類在預設情況下，傾向於將人類的特質與行為賦予動物、物體乃至科技等非人類實體。這種傾向稱為「擬人化」。我們之所以會對系統、物體或動物進行擬人化，並非因為真的相信它們具有人類特質，而是一種自動的認知反應——這讓我們能夠預測其行為，並引導自己與它互動。

People often anthropomorphise computer and AI systems by ascribing attributes like intention, personality, awareness (the capacity to feel or be conscious) and even sociality (the capacity to imitate human relationships or play a social role). These characteristics may not necessarily be designed into the system or its interface, but can be perceived by the user. Subtle visual cues and the interactivity of a system's interface can often encourage people to further anthropomorphize the system.

人們常常對電腦與 AI 系統進行擬人化，將意圖、個性、感知能力（感受或意識的能力），甚至社交性（模仿人際關係或扮演社交角色的能力）等特質賦予它們。這些特質不一定是系統或介面設計的一部分，但使用者會主觀感知到它們的存在。系統介面中微妙的視覺線索與互動性，往往會進一步強化使用者對系統的擬人化傾向。

---

### Humanization is the intentional addition of human-like traits to non-human entities
### 人類化是刻意將類人特質加入非人類實體的行為

Humanization is when a non-human object is purposefully imbued with human-like traits. This may be a structural property like a robotic hand or a human-like voice, or it may be gestural like an expression or a personality. Research has shown that when a system has human-like features that feel familiar to users (such as, if the system feels similar to another human being, or appears to have the same dimension of identity as the user), the system can be perceived as having more experience and agency.

人類化是指刻意將類人特質賦予非人類物體的過程。這可能是結構性的特質（如機械手臂或類人聲音），也可能是姿態性的（如表情或個性）。研究顯示，當系統具有讓使用者感到熟悉的類人特徵時（例如，系統讓人感覺與另一個人類相似，或看起來與使用者擁有相同的身份維度），使用者會認為這個系統具有更豐富的經驗與更強的能動性。

---

### AI systems are becoming much more natural at responding to multimodal human inputs
### AI 系統正在變得越來越自然地回應多模態人類輸入

While humanized interfaces have existed for many years, they have become much more natural at responding to complex human inputs across many modalities. This makes it easy to project human-like traits onto the AI systems — such as knowledge, memory, experience, semantic comprehension or even empathy.

人類化介面雖已存在多年，但現在它們在回應跨模態的複雜人類輸入方面變得更加自然。這使得人們更容易將類人特質投射到 AI 系統上——例如知識、記憶、經驗、語意理解乃至同理心。

Early versions of voice assistants were capable of performing a few well-defined tasks competently. Now, products powered by generative AI and language models afford a much more coherent experience. Users can interact with these products using written text and spoken word, in addition to typical UI components like drop-downs and buttons. Some products support rich, multimodal inputs and follow-up conversations that have made interactions even more natural and human-like.

早期版本的語音助理只能勝任少數定義明確的任務。現在，由生成式 AI 與語言模型驅動的產品提供了更為連貫的體驗。使用者除了可以使用下拉選單、按鈕等典型 UI 元件外，還可以透過文字和語音與這些產品互動。部分產品支援豐富的多模態輸入與後續對話，使互動體驗更加自然、更具人類感。

## Decide if Human-likeness Helps Users in Their Goals
## 決定人類化是否真的有助於使用者達成目標

---

### Mental models are simplifications that help us manage complexity across contexts
### 心智模型是幫助我們在不同情境中管理複雜性的簡化工具

People tend to form mental models that are simpler than reality, and so we tend to apply anthropomorphic mental models to humanized interfaces. For example, people will often respond to social cues from AI systems with social behaviors appropriate in a human-to-human setting, such as saying "thank you" to a voice assistant.

人們傾向於建立比現實更簡化的心智模型，因此我們會將擬人化的心智模型套用到人類化介面上。例如，人們往往會以人際互動中適當的社交行為，回應 AI 系統發出的社交線索——比如對語音助理說「謝謝」。

Humanized interfaces help establish relatability, reduce complexity, and give users a sense of control, comfort, and intuitive interaction patterns. However, they can easily encourage an inaccurate understanding of AI-powered products and cause usability issues or introduce risks to users. This is because AI systems execute tasks in ways that are inherently different from what you would expect from another person, even if the AI-powered functionality feels similar on the surface. When designing human-like characteristics into your AI system, consider the following:

人類化介面有助於建立親切感、降低複雜性，並賦予使用者掌控感、舒適感與直覺式的互動模式。然而，它們也很容易讓使用者對 AI 產品形成不準確的理解，進而造成可用性問題或引發風險。這是因為 AI 系統執行任務的方式，本質上與你對另一個人的期待大相逕庭，即便 AI 的功能在表面上感覺相似。在為 AI 系統設計類人特質時，請考慮以下幾點：

- **Comparison to human capabilities（與人類能力的比較）：** Generative AI models produce new content, so people will compare generative AI outputs to their own capabilities and skills – and in many cases, to the skills of other, more competent people. This can make evaluating your product experience subjective and challenging.
  生成式 AI 模型會生產新內容，因此人們會將 AI 的輸出與自身能力和技能進行比較——在許多情況下，還會與更有能力的他人進行比較。這會使評估產品體驗變得主觀且充滿挑戰。

- **Distrust and agency（不信任與能動性）：** Often we think that the idea of a generalized "helper AI" is easier to grasp and more inviting for users, but the risk of mistrust is high when the system's limits aren't clear. People might distrust the motivations of an AI product if it is introduced in a manner that suggests a sense of agency without clear principles or clear indication of tasks and actions that the system is restricted from performing.
  我們常認為「通用助手 AI」的概念更容易被使用者理解和接受，但當系統的限制不明確時，不信任的風險就會很高。如果 AI 產品的介紹方式暗示了某種能動性，卻沒有明確的原則或清楚說明系統被限制執行的任務與行為，使用者可能會懷疑 AI 的動機。

- **Offering too much information and over-reliance（提供過多資訊與過度依賴）：** When users attribute human-like capabilities to an AI system, they can sometimes disclose more information than they would otherwise, by offering more context, or using more goal-based language. This can create privacy risks, as people may not understand how this information is retained or used. They may also rely on the system more than they should, in place of help they might get from a qualified professional.
  當使用者將類人能力歸因於 AI 系統時，他們有時會提供比平常更多的資訊——提供更多背景脈絡，或使用更多目標導向的語言。這可能造成隱私風險，因為人們未必了解這些資訊如何被保存或使用。他們也可能過度依賴系統，取代了本應尋求合格專業人士的幫助。

- **Systemic social harms（系統性社會傷害）：** Human-like characteristics in AI outputs can also introduce sociotechnical harms for individuals, such as performing exceptionally well on certain subgroups and leaving out others, promoting narrow stereotypes, caricaturing, and for professionals, the possibility of displacement if the system seems as if it can meet or match human performance even if it can't.
  AI 輸出中的類人特質也可能對個人造成社會技術層面的傷害，例如：在某些子族群上表現特別優異而排斥其他群體、強化狹隘的刻板印象、諷刺描繪，以及對專業人士而言，若系統看似能達到甚至超越人類表現（即便實際上做不到），可能帶來被取代的威脅。

---

### A working mental model is not the same as an accurate mental model
### 能運作的心智模型，不等於準確的心智模型

Understanding how an AI-powered product executes a task should effectively allow a user to predict how the product will respond to their input: "If I do this, then it does this". This gives users greater comfort and confidence in using the product.

理解 AI 產品如何執行任務，應能讓使用者有效預測產品對其輸入的回應：「如果我這樣做，它就會那樣做」。這給了使用者更大的舒適感與使用信心。

However, a description like "Smart Photo Tagging" sounds like the tool is tagging photos the same way a person would, just "smart". If someone hasn't used this tool before, their mental model of this process is by default, a human one. The app may be able to find and tag all the pictures of a particular friend, just as a person would, but miss some that show them from the back. This is an unexpected break: of course most people can recognize their friends from multiple angles, but this tool can't.

然而，像「智慧照片標記」這樣的描述，聽起來像是工具以人類的方式標記照片，只是更「智慧」。如果使用者以前沒有用過這個工具，他們對這個流程的心智模型，預設就是人類的那一種。這個應用程式或許能像人一樣找出並標記某位朋友的所有照片，但卻可能漏掉從背後拍攝的照片。這是一個出乎意料的落差：大多數人當然可以從各種角度認出朋友，但這個工具做不到。

A disconnect like this doesn't necessarily mean that the product itself or the mental model is broken. The key is to communicate the system's limits and capabilities in a way that doesn't create or support unwarranted expectations. AI doesn't work the same way people do, so though the mental model is convenient, the product's underlying behavior is very different from humans.

這樣的落差並不一定意味著產品本身或心智模型出了問題。關鍵在於以不會製造或強化不合理期待的方式，向使用者傳達系統的限制與能力。AI 的運作方式與人類不同，因此儘管這種心智模型使用起來方便，但產品的底層行為與人類實際上大相逕庭。

When users can't accurately map the system's abilities, they may over-trust the system at the wrong times, or miss out on the greatest value-add of all: better ways to do a task they take for granted.

當使用者無法準確理解系統的能力邊界時，他們可能在錯誤的時機過度信任系統，或錯失最大的價值所在：以更好的方式完成他們習以為常的任務。

## Recommendations: Clarify Humanization of AI Products
## 建議：釐清 AI 產品的人類化程度

---

### Make it clear that the product is AI-powered
### 明確告知使用者產品由 AI 驅動

It's critical to disclose when your product is powered by AI to mitigate potential risks. Your messages should explicitly state that the product is not a human, in a way that's accessible to all users regardless of age, technical literacy, education level, or physical ability. Institute the necessary safeguards and explanations so user groups who are particularly vulnerable to misunderstanding AI responses as humans can discern outputs that are generated by AI.

當產品由 AI 驅動時，揭露這一點對於降低潛在風險至關重要。你的訊息應明確說明產品不是人類，且這樣的表達方式應讓所有使用者都能理解——無論年齡、技術素養、教育程度或身體能力如何。建立必要的防護措施與說明，使特別容易將 AI 回應誤認為人類的使用群體，能夠辨別出哪些是 AI 生成的輸出。

---

### Create controls that help people concretely manage the AI system
### 建立讓使用者能具體操控 AI 系統的控制機制

Where possible, provide additional controls to steer and explore the AI system. Interacting with a controllable system can help people establish cause-and-effect relationships between their inputs and human-like AI outputs.

在可能的情況下，提供額外的控制機制，讓使用者能夠引導與探索 AI 系統。與可控系統的互動，有助於使用者在自身的輸入與 AI 的類人輸出之間，建立因果關係的認知。

---

### Help people calibrate their belief and trust in AI outcomes for real world tasks
### 幫助使用者在現實任務中校準對 AI 結果的信任程度

A recommendation from a human will be perceived very differently from a machine-generated one. Offer cues to help users decide how much they can trust an AI system's outputs, and which parts of the output might be useful for decision making.

來自人類的建議與機器生成的建議，會被使用者感知為截然不同的東西。提供線索幫助使用者判斷他們能在多大程度上信任 AI 系統的輸出，以及輸出中哪些部分可能有助於決策。

## Multi-turn Interactions & Sociality
## 多輪互動與社交性

---

### Factor in interactions and behaviors that stem from sociality and collaboration
### 將源自社交性與協作的互動行為納入考量

Leveraging human characteristics to build mental models is particularly useful if your product interactions rely on distinctly human behaviors, such as conversation. But human-like interactions and behaviors can introduce specific expectations around collaboration.

如果你的產品互動依賴對話等明確的人類行為，那麼運用人類特質來建構心智模型會特別有用。但類人的互動與行為，也可能帶入特定的協作期待。

For example, a human conversational partner will bring their unique perspectives and lived experience to the conversation. In addition, they'll take initiative, keep track of shared knowledge, and have a keen sense of context and culture that drives successful interactions and collaboration. Throughout the conversation, they'll naturally offer cues that they've understood what's needed of them, or will ask for clarifications. AI-driven conversational experiences may not be able to meet these expectations, or may not be able to respond to some styles of interrogation. If a conversational experience does not set up this context, people may not know what or how they can ask, and what kinds of answers to expect.

舉例來說，人類的對話夥伴會將其獨特的視角與生活經驗帶入對話。此外，他們會主動出擊、追蹤共享知識，並擁有對語境和文化的敏銳感知，推動成功的互動與協作。在整個對話過程中，他們會自然地給出已理解對方需求的訊號，或主動尋求澄清。AI 驅動的對話體驗可能無法滿足這些期待，也可能無法回應某些風格的提問方式。如果對話體驗沒有預先建立好這樣的情境脈絡，使用者可能不知道可以問什麼、怎麼問，以及可以期待什麼樣的答案。

In multi-turn experiences, such as conversational experiences or those with quick follow up actions or iterations, people can benefit when the AI system offers some initiative to help them understand what's possible or likely to be helpful in that moment. Consider offering interaction cues to help people understand:

在多輪體驗中（如對話體驗，或需要快速後續操作或迭代的情境），當 AI 系統主動提供線索，幫助使用者了解當前什麼是可能的、什麼是有幫助的，使用者能從中獲益。考慮提供互動線索，幫助使用者理解以下幾點：

- **Input templates（輸入模板）：** Show people how to formulate an input for your AI-driven interface, such as offering a suggestion that users can build from.
  向使用者展示如何為你的 AI 介面構建輸入，例如提供一個使用者可以在此基礎上延伸的建議。

- **Clarifications（澄清）：** If a person underspecifies in an input, ask questions or suggest additions to refine their input or request.
  如果使用者的輸入過於模糊，提問或建議補充內容，以精煉其輸入或請求。

- **Obstacles（障礙）：** When the request is beyond the scope of the model, show an error message and ask for rich feedback.
  當請求超出模型的處理範疇時，顯示錯誤訊息並徵求詳細的回饋。

- **Pointers（指引）：** If a person is on a productive path, suggest follow ups, or recommend next steps.
  如果使用者走在有成效的方向上，建議後續步驟或推薦下一步行動。

- **Acknowledgement（確認）：** For complex inputs, summarize the task and context from the input back to the user.
  對於複雜的輸入，將任務與背景脈絡整理後向使用者確認。

---

### Humanization can alter perceptions and expectations
### 人類化會改變使用者的感知與期待

Using common anthropomorphic cues can help people intuitively understand how to use your system. It's much easier for people to understand that they can talk to something that invites them to do so in a conversational way. Linguistic features like pleasantries typical to social conversations ("Please", "Thank you"), disfluencies and expressions of uncertainty ("Uhh", "I'm not sure but…"), and character-based personas can increase anthropomorphism.

使用常見的擬人化線索，有助於使用者直覺地理解如何使用你的系統。對於那些以對話方式邀請互動的事物，人們更容易理解自己可以與之交談。社交對話中常見的禮貌用語（「請」、「謝謝」）、口語停頓與不確定性的表達（「呃」、「我不確定，但……」），以及角色化的人格設定，都能提升擬人化程度。

Regardless of whether it is warranted, trust in an AI system can grow when it exhibits increasingly anthropomorphic behavior, which can be disproportionately problematic for vulnerable populations. Heightened trust, reliance, or belief in the system outputs can lead to over-estimating the capabilities of your system, which can increase the rate of errors and failure.

無論是否合理，當 AI 系統展現出越來越多的擬人化行為時，使用者對其的信任往往會增長——這對弱勢族群而言可能帶來不成比例的問題。對系統輸出過度的信任、依賴或信念，會導致使用者高估系統的能力，進而提高錯誤與失敗的發生率。

On the other hand, adding anthropomorphic cues or human-like attributes to expert or professional AI tools can lead to under-estimating the capabilities of the tool, regardless of how well it performs or the value it brings to the user. In these cases, human-likeness can suggest a lack of controllability, increased effort, and lower confidence in outcomes, which can be discouraging for experts. If a system presents itself as having expertise, this can lead to users over-trusting the system, which can lead to false or inaccurate work products, or worse, put them or their customers in harm's way.

另一方面，在專業或行業 AI 工具中加入擬人化線索或類人特質，無論工具表現多好或為使用者帶來多少價值，都可能導致使用者低估工具的能力。在這些情況下，類人特質可能暗示著缺乏可控性、需要更多努力，以及對結果信心不足，這對專業人士而言可能造成挫折。如果系統展現出具有專業知識的樣貌，可能導致使用者過度信任系統，進而產生錯誤或不準確的工作成果，更嚴重的是危害使用者或其客戶的安全。

In short, presenting the AI responses as an expert opinion without allowing users to inspect how that opinion is formed is not recommended. Instead, try presenting outcomes as a curated or ranked set of viewpoints with citations and attributions or verifiable facts. Consider which anthropomorphic cues from your AI system people might pick up on, and consider if these need to occur by design or avoided. It's a delicate balance between cueing the right type of interaction while trying to limit the level of mismatched expectations or failures.

簡而言之，將 AI 的回應呈現為專家意見，卻不讓使用者檢視這個意見是如何形成的，是不建議的做法。相反地，嘗試將結果呈現為一組經過篩選或排序的觀點，附上引用來源或可驗證的事實。思考使用者可能會從你的 AI 系統中感知到哪些擬人化線索，並評估這些線索是應該刻意設計，還是需要加以避免。在引導正確的互動類型與限制期望落差或失敗的程度之間，取得精妙的平衡，是一門藝術。

## Recommendations: Decide How Human-like Your System Should Be
## 建議：決定你的系統應具備多高程度的人類化

---

### Consider if the use case of your product requires humanization
### 評估你的產品使用情境是否需要人類化

Not all AI powered systems need to be human-like. Consider if you need to add human-like attributes to your interfaces, if at all. If you do, actively remind the user the system is not human.

並非所有 AI 驅動的系統都需要具備類人特質。評估你是否需要在介面中加入類人特質，甚至是否需要加入。如果確實加入了，請主動提醒使用者這個系統並非人類。

---

### Honor expectations of social competence
### 尊重使用者對社交能力的期待

People will naturally project social expectations onto AI products. If humanization will significantly help your users make progress towards their goals, choose the level of humanization based on how well your AI's capabilities match the user's perceptions of what a human can do.

人們自然會將社交期待投射到 AI 產品上。如果人類化能夠顯著幫助使用者朝目標邁進，那麼應根據 AI 能力與使用者對「人類能做什麼」之感知的吻合程度，選擇適當的人類化程度。

---

### Evaluate how humanization will change expectations of performance and competence
### 評估人類化將如何改變使用者對表現與能力的期待

Humanizing an expert tool can create incorrect assumptions and increase misconceptions about the tool's competence, performance, and utility. In some cases, such as when the user is performing cognitively demanding tasks, adding anthropomorphic cues can be distracting.

將專業工具人類化，可能會製造不正確的假設，並增加對工具能力、表現與實用性的誤解。在某些情況下——例如使用者正在執行高認知負荷的任務時——加入擬人化線索反而可能造成干擾。

---

### Humanization is the result of a sum of many interactions
### 人類化是眾多互動元素累積的結果

It's worth remembering that human-likeness is rarely the result of a single UI decision. Rather, many interactions and UI elements contribute to people anthropomorphizing a system. Consider if UI elements that contribute to anthropomorphization change the way users interact with your product; and if this detracts from their goals.

值得謹記的是，類人感很少是單一 UI 決策的結果。相反地，許多互動與 UI 元素共同促成了使用者對系統的擬人化。評估那些促進擬人化的 UI 元素，是否改變了使用者與你產品的互動方式；以及這是否有悖於他們的目標。
